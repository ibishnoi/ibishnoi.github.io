Talks and Conferences

By Indra Bishnoi | December 9, 2020

Developmental Disabilities Research Day

In May of 2018, I was given the opportunity to give an oral presentation for Developmental Disabilities Research Day at Western University.

This was the first oral presentation I had ever done and it was right at the start of my MSc! When I first heard that I was selected for an oral presentation for this event, I didn’t feel prepared at all. However, a lab mate at the time gave me the confidence I needed (and helped me practice of course)!

The day of, I was pretty nervous, but it went so well! I got some great questions which really set the stage for my MSc.

Kiwanis Senior’s Centre

The Kiwanis Senior’s Centre is a senior’s centre here in London, ON. They have partnered with Western University, specifically Retiring With Strong Minds to bring new research right to seniors in our community. I had the chance to present here in September 2018.

Presenting here was an amazing experience. It taught me a lot about how to communicate my research to a lay audience, as I quite literally had to explain everything like I was explaining it to my grandma!

The most memorable and heartwarming moment I shared with the audience here was when an audience member enthusiastically exclaimed that “[she] will be checking the ingredients list of all of [her] grandchildren’s bread and dairy products,” as the drug I spoke about during my task is used as a preservative in these products.
